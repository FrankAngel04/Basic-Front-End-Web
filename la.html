Los Angeles

A City in California

City's population and year it was incorporated

As of 2023, Los Angeles has a population of approximately 3.8 million people. The city experienced a peak population of around 3.98 million in 2019. The population slightly declined between 2020 and 2022.

Region of the state in which the City is located

Los Angeles is located in Southern California. It is known for its warm summer and cool winter climates, ethnic diversity, and Hollywood, a symbolic landmark for the entertainment and film industry.

Classification of the City

Los Angeles is classified as an urban city. It is the largest city in California and the second-largest metropolitan in the United States.

Average income level of the City

The average income level in Los Angeles is $98,200 USD (2023) where the state average is $78,000.

Top 3 attractions in the City

Los Angeles is also often known as the City of Angels. It is a city that is rich in culture, history, and entertainment. From the hills of Hollywood to the coastal beaches of Malibu, Santa Monica, and Venice, Los Angeles offers unique sights and experiences. Below is a list of some of the city's most famous attractions:

  1. The Hollywood Walk of Fame: A tribute to celebrities in the entertainment industry, featuring celebrity brass stars embedded into the sidewalks.
  2. Los Angeles County Museum of Art (LACMA): The largest and one of many musueums in the city showcasing an extensive collection of artwork.
  3. Santa Monica Pier: Famous for its bohemian ambience, lively boardwalk featuring a ferris wheel and rollercoaster, and an array of street performers.
