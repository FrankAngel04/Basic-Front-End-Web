Sacramento

The Capital of California

Capital's population and year it was incorporated

Sacramento is the capital city of California, a vibrant urban center with a rich history and diverse community. As of 2023, the city's population is approximately 528,306, presenting a modest growth in number since the 2020 census. Sacramento officially incorporated in 1850, making it as a key city in California's development.

Region of the state in which the capital is located

Geographically, Sacramento is located in Northern California. It serves as a central hub for the state government while surrounded by the diverse sceneries of Northern California.

Classification of the Capital

Sacramento is predominantly urban, with its dense population and role as a state capital to be metropolitan. It also mixes in elements of suburban development, usually in the outskirts of the city, where many residential neighborhoods combine the surrounding valleys.

Average income level of the Capital

The median household income in the city stands at around $80,254, about 90 percent of the state's average, slightly below the state's average of $91,551.
